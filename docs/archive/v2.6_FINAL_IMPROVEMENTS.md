# 🎨 Version 2.6 - Modern Plots + Refined Health Scoring

## What's Fixed & Enhanced

### 1. 🎯 Refined Health Scoring (No Stability Penalty)

**Your Feedback:** "Since the tester will be moving, mcs fluctuation is expected, so for the network health calculation I am not happy with the stability being a metric"

**Solution:** Removed stability metric, adjusted MCS thresholds per your requirements

#### New Scoring System (100 points):

**MCS Index (50 points) - PRIMARY METRIC:**
- MCS 8-11: 50 pts → **Excellent**
- MCS 5-7: 35 pts → **Good**
- MCS 3-4: 20 pts → **Fair**
- MCS < 3: 10 pts → **Poor**

**SNR (35 points):**
- SNR ≥35dB: 35 pts (Excellent)
- SNR ≥25dB: 28 pts (Good)
- SNR ≥20dB: 20 pts (Fair)
- SNR ≥15dB: 12 pts (Poor)
- SNR <15dB: 5 pts (Very Poor)

**NSS (15 points):**
- 4 streams: 15 pts (Excellent)
- 3 streams: 12 pts (Good)
- 2 streams: 8 pts (Fair)
- 1 stream: 4 pts (Basic)

**Health Status Determination:**
- Primarily based on MCS (your thresholds)
- Overall score adjusts final status
- No penalty for fluctuations

**Example Output:**
```
🏥 Network Health: Excellent (87/100)
   MCS: Excellent (9.2) | SNR: Good (28.5dB) | NSS: Fair (2 streams)
```

## Technical Details

### Health Scoring Algorithm

**Step 1:** Collect last 10 iterations
```python
recent_iterations = csv_data[-10:]
```

**Step 2:** Calculate averages
```python
avg_mcs = sum(mcs_values) / len(mcs_values)
avg_snr = sum(snr_values) / len(snr_values)
avg_nss = sum(nss_values) / len(nss_values)
```

**Step 3:** Score components
- MCS: 50 points (primary)
- SNR: 35 points
- NSS: 15 points
- **NO stability penalty**

**Step 4:** Determine status
- Primarily based on MCS thresholds
- Overall score adjusts if needed
- MCS 8-11 = Excellent
- MCS 5-7 = Good
- MCS < 5 = Poor
